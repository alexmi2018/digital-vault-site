Privacy Policy for Digital Vault

Last updated: June 11, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Digital Vault handles vault data, backup and restore, Google services, and related features. Digital Vault is designed to store vault data locally on your device.

1. Overview

Digital Vault is an Android app for storing passwords, private records, and related security details. The app is designed so your vault stays on your device unless you choose an optional encrypted backup.

2. What the app stores on your device

The app stores your vault content on your device in encrypted form. This can include passwords, private records, recovery information, and other data you save in the app. Vault content is not collected by the developer.

3. Master password

Your master password is used on your device to unlock the vault. It is not sent to the developer through the app or this website.

4. Google Drive backup and restore

Google Drive backup and restore is optional and user-controlled. If you choose to use it, the app creates an encrypted backup file before upload. The backup is stored in your Google Drive only if you approve the backup flow.

5. Google services

The app may use Google services when you sign in to Google, use Drive backup or restore, install the app from Google Play, or use billing features. Those services may process data according to Google's own policies. Digital Vault does not control Google's separate services or their privacy practices.

6. Google Play Billing and Pro features

Google Play Billing may be used for Pro features when those features are enabled. Purchase and billing data may be processed by Google and by the Google Play system according to their policies. If Pro features are not enabled, this section may not apply to your use of the app.

7. Password health and compromise checks

Digital Vault may review saved entries to help you spot weak, reused, or potentially compromised passwords. These checks are intended to provide security insights without exposing your actual passwords in site text or public summaries.

8. Import from Google Password Manager CSV

The app may support importing password data from a Google Password Manager CSV file. Any imported file is handled locally on your device as part of the import process. You should review imports carefully before saving them into your vault.

9. Data deletion

You can delete local app data by uninstalling Digital Vault or by clearing the app's data in your device settings.

If you use Google Drive cloud backup, encrypted backup files may be stored in a hidden app-specific area of your Google Drive account associated with Digital Vault. These files may not appear as regular files in your Google Drive.

Disconnecting Google Drive in the app removes the local connection from that device and stops future backup or restore actions from that connection. It does not automatically delete encrypted backup files that were already stored in your Google Drive account.

To delete cloud backup data stored for Digital Vault, open Google Drive settings, go to Manage apps, find Digital Vault, open its options, and delete its hidden app data.

Deleting cloud backup data from Google Drive does not automatically delete vault data that is already stored locally on your device.

10. Children's privacy

Digital Vault is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children through this site or the app.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect product or policy changes. When we do, we will update the date at the top of this page.

12. Contact

If you have questions about this policy, contact us at digitalvault.app@gmail.com.
